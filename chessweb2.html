Chess Fundamentals

Step 1: Assembling the chess board.

When setting up a chess board at the start of the game you want a white square to be in the lower right corner for every player.

The chess pieces are always positioned in the same manner. There are pawns in the second row. The rooks are placed in the corners, followed by the knights who stand next to them, the bishops, the queen, who always wears her own complementary color (black queen on black, white queen on white), and the king on the last square.

Step 2: Moving the chess pieces.

How to move the Pawn

With the exception of their initial move, which allows them to advance two squares, pawns can only advance one square at a time.Even though they can only travel forward, they are only able to capture forward diagonally. They are incapable of sliding backwards or capture backwards diagonally. If a piece is in front of them they are unable to move past.

Step 3:Ultilizing the special moves in chess.

Promoting a Pawn in chess.

With the exception of a king, a pawn can be promoted to any other chess piece once it reaches the opposite side of the board.

The "En Passant"

Another special abilty a pawn can do is called the "en passant" which stands for "in passing" in french. When a pawn moves two squares on it's first move, and by doing so lands to the side of an opponent's pawns, that other pawn has the option of capturing the first pawn as it passes by.

How to castle in chess.

When it comes to playing chess "castling" is the most used move. This move allows you to do two critical things that will help you win most games:put your king in a safer position and get your rook out of the corner into the game.When castling the rook advances to the square the king recently crossed after the king progresses two spaces in the direction of a rook.You need to fulfill the following requirements in order to lawfully castle: The rook and the king have never moved before. There are no pieces between the king and the rook. At the moment, the king is not in check. The king must not pass through a square that is under attack by enemy pieces The king must not end up in check

Step 4:Who makes the first move in chess.

The player with the white chess pieces moves first always. Being able to move first gives a player a slight advantage since they're able to start attacking first.

Step 5:How to win a game of chess.

Chess games can be ended in a variety of ways, including checkmate, draw, resignation, and forfeit on time.
